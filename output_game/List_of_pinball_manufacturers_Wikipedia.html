From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This is a partial list of pinball manufacturers of past and present organized alphabetically by name. The article only includes producers of pinball machines at least in a small series which excludes makers of single unit custom pinball machines.

Present

[edit]
  • American Pinball (founded 2015)[1]
  • Barrels of Fun (founded 2021)[2]
  • Chicago Gaming Company (founded 2001)[3]
  • Dutch Pinball (founded 2010)[4]
  • Hexa Pinball (founded 2023)[4]
  • Homepin Taiwan Co. Ltd. (founded 2013)[5]
  • Jersey Jack Pinball (founded 2011)[6]
  • Multimorphic Inc (founded 2009)[7]
  • Pawlowski Pinball (founded 2023)[8]
  • Pedretti Gaming (founded 1976; pinball manufacturing 2024)[9]
    • Euro Pinball Corp (a co-venture with Pinball Brothers that manufactures their machines; founded 2023)[10]
  • Phenix Pinball (founded 2018)[4]
  • Pinball Adventures (founded 2018)[8]
  • Pinball Brothers (founded 2018)[4]
    • Euro Pinball Corp (a co-venture with Pedretti Gaming that manufactures their machines; founded 2023)[10]
  • Quetzal Pinball (founded 2012)[4]
  • Spooky Pinball (founded 2013)[11]
  • Stern Pinball (founded 1999)[12]
    • As DataEast (1986-1994)
    • As Sega Pinball (1994-1999)
  • Team Pinball (founded 2018)[4]
  • TiltBob Pinball (founded 2023)[13]
  • Turner Pinball (founded 2022)[14]
  • Vector Pinball (founded 2022)
  • Wonderland Amusements (founded 2024)[15]

Past

[edit]
  • A. Hankin & Company (1978-1980)[16]
  • Allied Leisure
  • Alvin G & Co. (1991-1994)[17]
  • Astro Games
  • Atari (1972-1992; pinball manufacturing 1976-1979)[18]
  • Automatic Industries Inc. (1931-1936)[19]
  • Bally Manufacturing (1932-1988)[6]
  • Bill Port (1976-1982)[20]
  • Capcom Coin-Op, a subsidiary of Capcom
  • Centro Matic
  • Coffee-Mat
  • Chicago Coin (1932-1977; assets sold to Stern Electronics, Inc.)[12]
  • Data East (1986-1994; sold to Sega Pinball)[12]
  • deeproot (2015-2021)[21][22] (note: no pinball machine was ever produced)
  • Exhibit Supply Company (1901-1979; pinball manufacturing 1932-1957)[23]
  • Fascination Int., Inc (Allied Leisure)
  • Game Plan
  • Genco (1931-1958)[24]
  • Gottlieb (1927-1996)[6]
  • Haggis Pinball (2021-2024)[25]
  • Heighway Pinball (2012-2018)[4]
  • Inder (1971-1993)[26]
  • InterFlip/Recreativos Franco
  • J.H. Keeney & Co.
  • Jennings & Company
  • Jeutel pinball
  • LTD of Brazil
  • MAC S.A.
  • Nordamatic
  • Nuova Bell/Bell Games
  • Maresa (1960-1976)[27]
  • MarsaPlay (2010-2013)[28]
  • Mirco Games[29]
  • Midway Games
  • Peyper (1977-1994)[27]
  • Pinstar
  • Playmatic
  • Rally Play
  • Petaco[27]
    • As Petaco (domestic brand name) (1962-1992)
    • As Recel (international brand name) (1971-1996)
    • As Sleic (1992-1996)
  • Ripepi
  • Sega Pinball (1994-1999; sold to Stern Pinball)[12]
  • Sega, S.A. SONIC
  • Spinball S.A.L.
  • Stern Electronics (1977-1985)[12]
  • SunCoast Pinball
  • Taito of Brazil (1972-1985)[30]
  • Talleres del Llobregat (estimated 1960s-1973)[27]
  • tecnoplay
  • The Valley Company
  • United Manufacturing
  • Viza
  • WhizBang Pinball (2011-2017)[31][32]
  • Wico
  • Williams Electronics / WMS Industries (1943-1999)[6]
  • Zaccaria (1974-1988)[33]
    • As Mr. Game (1988-1990)[34]
  • Zidware (2011-2017)[35]
