Wario video games have been developed by several companies, including Nintendo, Suzak, Good-Feel, Treasure and Intelligent Systems, for Nintendo video game consoles and handhelds dating from the Game Boy. The first game to feature Wario was Super Mario Land 2: 6 Golden Coins, as the antagonist; Wario Land: Super Mario Land 3 was the first to feature Wario as a playable character.[1][2]

The Wario video game franchise consists largely of two separate components: a series of platform games primarily using the Wario Land branding, and a series of party games under the WarioWare moniker.[citation needed] The changes from the Super Mario Land series, both stylistically and storywise—make the Wario Land games unique from other platformers in the genre.[failed verification][3][4] The WarioWare games are minigame compilations in which the player is required to perform a series of short activities at a quickening pace, with the player having four lives.[5][6][needs update?] Wario games also include occasional games of other genres such as Wario's Woods and Mario & Wario.
