Telenet Japan Co., Ltd. (株式会社日本テレネット, Kabushiki-gaisha Nihon Terenetto) was a Japanese video game and software developer founded in October 1983 by Kazuyuki Fukushima.[1] The company had several video game divisions including: Wolf Team, Laser Soft, Renovation Game, Renovation Products, and Riot.[2]

History

[edit]

The company was founded in October 1983 by Kazuyuki Fukushima.[3] The first division created was Wolf Team, which was created in 1986. The Riot division came into existence in 1991 when Telenet Japan was expanding in the country.[citation needed]

However, because Telenet was starting to lose sales in 1993, the company went through extensive restructuring which resulted in the closing of a few subsidiaries. Some staff employed at Laser Soft and Riot were transferred to another subsidiary, Wolfteam. The same year, several key developers of the PC Engine games Tenshi no Uta I & II left Riot to found Media.Vision and work on a new RPG franchise, Wild Arms.[citation needed]

With a debt of ¥1 billion, the company ceased operating in late September 2007 and closed its doors on October 25.[4] According to the Tokyo Keizai, the company ran into debt in selling pachinko game software, which made the company get into debt due to the US branches failing.[5]

Sunsoft acquired Telenet's entire software library in December 2009, citing plans to remake or re-release (via Virtual Console) the old titles.[6] The Japanese company Edia acquired Telenet's catalogue from City Connection in January 2020.[7]

Organization

[edit]

Telenet Japan was formerly made up of the following teams: [2]

Lasersoft

[edit]

Served as Telenet's publishing arm for optical media, such as PC Engine. It was later merged with Wolf Team.

Micro World

[edit]

Micro World served as Telenet's publishing arm to localize and market non-Japanese games.[8]

Wolf Team

[edit]
Main article: Namco Tales Studio

Renovation Games

[edit]

Telenet Japan's Japanese publishing arm.

Renovation Products

[edit]

Telenet Japan's North American subsidiary, Renovation Products, was acquired by Sega of America in 1993.[9] It was formerly located in California.[10]

Riot

[edit]

Riot was also known for employing graphic artist and later game director Eiji Kikuchi, as well as music composer Michiko Naruke.[citation needed] It's sometimes known as Reno.[1]
