Bikeway in Mexico City

The Ciclovía La Gran Tenochtitlán is an under-construction bikeway in central Mexico City. It runs 34 kilometers (21 mi) along Calzada de Tlalpan, a major north–south thoroughfare, starting at Plaza Tlaxcoaque, in the historic center of the city, toward the Estadio Azteca area.[1] The bikeway was announced on 3 June 2025 by the head of government of Mexico City, Clara Brugada,[2] and, although incomplete,[3] was inaugurated on 19 April 2026. In her remarks at the event, Brugada highlighted its connection with multiple Mexico City Metro stations.[4] It has drawn significant criticism due to its design and zoning.

Overview

[edit]

The Ciclovía La Gran Tenochtitlán is divided in two sections. From Tlaxcoaque to Chabacano metro station, it will run along a 1.8-kilometer (1.1 mi) overpass[5] that is under construction, and which will also serve as a pedestrian park;[6] after that station, it is to run as a bike lane to CETRAM Huipulco, near the stadium.[7] The overpass will feature rainwater collectors with planters and will be covered with recycled PET. The bikeway will be 5 meters (16 ft) wide, with a 50-centimeter (20 in) shoulder, and will include two paths measuring 1.40 meters (4 ft 7 in) each.[8]

Criticism

[edit]

The bikeway received criticism from residents because it is intended for tourists ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup soccer games, with some sectors of the population calling it "social cleansing", and sex workers stating that it has displaced them, as Calzada de Tlalpan is an area with a concentration of sex workers.[9] Local vendors estimated a decline in sales during construction.[10] Motorists criticized the reduction by two lanes for the construction, which would cause traffic congestion.[11]

San Antonio Abad metro station had to be rebuilt because of the bridge.[12] The Mexico City Metro labor union questioned the project, describing it as "cosmetic", indicating that tourists will not primarily visit the Estadio Azteca by bicycle, and stating that it would eventually lead to subsidence, leaks, seepage, and damage to tracks and infrastructure of Line 2.[13]
