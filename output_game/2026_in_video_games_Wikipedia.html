In the video game industry, 2026 continues trends resulting from the large-scale layoffs from previous years, as well as impacts on hardware and consoles due to limitations on computer memory availability.


Major events

[edit]
Month Day(s) Event
January 13 Meta announced the closure of the studios Armature Studio, Sanzaru Games, and Twisted Pixel. In addition, they announced more than 1,000 jobs would be laid off in their Reality Labs business.[1][2]
21 Ubisoft announced a major restructuring into five creative houses.[3]
February 10–12 The Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences hosted the 2026 D.I.C.E. Summit and the 29th Annual D.I.C.E. Awards at the Aria Resort and Casino in Las Vegas, Nevada.[4]
20 Microsoft Gaming's Phil Spencer and Sarah Bond left the company, with Asha Sharma replacing Spencer, and Matt Booty promoted to chief content officer.[5]
March 9–21 The Game Developers Conference, branded as "Festival of Gaming", was held in San Francisco, California.[6]
24 Epic Games announced plans to lay off more than 1,000 employees in part to reduced Fortnite engagement.[7]
April 23 Microsoft Gaming rebranded as Xbox with a renewed approach to system exclusivity.[8]
June 5–8 Summer Game Fest 2026 is scheduled to be held at the Dolby Theatre in Los Angeles.[9]
August 26–30 Gamescom 2026 is scheduled to be held in Cologne, Germany.[10]
September 12–13 Blizzard Entertainment will host BlizzCon 2026 at Anaheim Convention Center in Anaheim, California.[11]
17–21 Tokyo Game Show 2026 is scheduled to be held at the Makuhari Messe in Chiba, Japan.[12]
December 10 The Game Awards 2026 is scheduled to be held at the Peacock Theater in Los Angeles.[13]

Notable deaths

[edit]
  • February 10 – Shutaro Iida (a.k.a. Curry the Kid), 52, one of the main programmers of various Castlevania titles and the creative director of Bloodstained: Ritual of the Night and its upcoming prequel — Bloodstained: The Scarlet Engagement.[14]
  • February 13 – Hideki Sato [ja], 75, Japanese businessman and engineer, recognized as major contributor to most of Sega's consoles.[15]
  • February 16 – Robert Duvall, 95, actor, best known for portraying Tom Hagen in The Godfather and its video game adaptation.[16]
  • March 17 - Yoran Heling (a.k.a. Yorhel), creator and maintainer of the Visual Novel Database.[17]
  • April 2 - Yoshihisa Kishimoto, 64, game developer and creator of the Kunio-kun and Double Dragon series.[18]
