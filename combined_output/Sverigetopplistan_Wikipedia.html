Sverigetopplistan (Swedish: [ˈsvæ̂rjɛˌtɔpːlɪstan], lit. "the Sweden top list") is the Swedish national record chart, based on sales data from IFPI Sverige.[1] It was formerly known as Topplistan (1975–1997) and Hitlistan (1998–2007) and has been known by its current name since October 2007. Before Topplistan, music sales in Sweden were recorded by Kvällstoppen,[2] whose weekly chart was a combined albums and singles list.

History

[edit]

For the period of 1976 to 2006, the official Swedish music charts were published by Sveriges Radio P3, a station owned by Sveriges Radio. At the end of 2006, it stopped publishing the general charts, which were entrusted to Swedish Recording Industry Association in the beginning of 2007. However, Sveriges Radio P3 continued to publish the most downloaded music charts, according to the statistics compiled by Nielsen SoundScan. The new strictly-download chart was called DigiListan.[citation needed]

Since 2006, the chart has included legal downloads.[3] The charts became the first in the world to include music streaming with singles (29 October 2010)[4] and then with albums (2013).[5]

From 14 November 1975 to 8 September 1993 the chart was only published every 2 weeks.[citation needed]

The chart is published every Friday, covering sales of the previous week from Friday to Thursday.[6]
